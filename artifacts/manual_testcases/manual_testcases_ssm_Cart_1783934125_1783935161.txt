Screen: Cart

Test Case 1: Verify Cart screen loads with all key cart components
Description: Validate that the Cart screen presents the expected UI elements required for reviewing items before checkout.
Steps:
  1. Navigate to the Cart screen.
  2. Observe the top section of the screen.
  3. Verify the presence of the "Cart title".
  4. Verify the "Cart items" list is displayed.
  5. For at least one listed item, verify that "Quantity controls" are visible.
  6. Verify that a "Remove item" button is available for cart items.
  7. Verify that the "Subtotal" text is displayed.
  8. Verify that the "Checkout" button is displayed.
Expected Result:
  - The Cart screen opens successfully.
  - "Cart title", "Cart items", "Quantity controls", "Remove item", "Subtotal", and "Checkout" are visible as applicable.
  - The screen is usable for cart review and checkout preparation.

Test Case 2: Verify cart items list supports scrolling when multiple items exist
Description: Ensure the "Cart items" list can be scrolled to review all products added to the cart.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with multiple items in the cart.
  2. Swipe up within the "Cart items" list.
  3. Continue scrolling until lower items become visible.
  4. Swipe down to return toward the top of the list.
Expected Result:
  - The "Cart items" list scrolls smoothly.
  - Additional cart items become visible when scrolling down the list.
  - Returning upward restores earlier visible items without layout issues.

Test Case 3: Verify increasing item quantity using Quantity controls
Description: Validate that the quantity stepper increases the selected item's quantity and updates cart totals accordingly.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with at least one item present.
  2. Note the current quantity for an item and the current "Subtotal".
  3. Tap the plus/increase option in the "Quantity controls" for that item.
  4. Observe the updated quantity shown for the item.
  5. Observe the updated "Subtotal".
Expected Result:
  - The item quantity increases by one.
  - The cart reflects the updated quantity immediately.
  - The "Subtotal" increases appropriately based on the item's price.

Test Case 4: Verify decreasing item quantity using Quantity controls
Description: Validate that the quantity stepper decreases the selected item's quantity and updates cart totals accordingly.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with an item whose quantity is greater than 1.
  2. Note the current quantity and the current "Subtotal".
  3. Tap the minus/decrease option in the "Quantity controls" for that item.
  4. Observe the updated quantity shown for the item.
  5. Observe the updated "Subtotal".
Expected Result:
  - The item quantity decreases by one.
  - The cart reflects the updated quantity immediately.
  - The "Subtotal" decreases appropriately based on the item's price.

Test Case 5: Verify quantity cannot be reduced below the minimum allowed value
Description: Confirm the cart prevents invalid quantity reduction, such as decreasing below 1 if the item remains in cart.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with an item quantity set to 1.
  2. Tap the minus/decrease option in the "Quantity controls" for that item.
  3. Observe the item quantity and cart state.
Expected Result:
  - The quantity does not go below the minimum allowed value.
  - If the app uses direct removal instead of zero quantity, the item remains at 1 until explicitly removed using "Remove item".
  - No incorrect negative or zero quantity is displayed unless supported by design.

Test Case 6: Verify removing a single item from the cart
Description: Ensure that tapping "Remove item" deletes the selected product from the cart and refreshes the subtotal.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with at least two items in the cart.
  2. Note the current number of items in the "Cart items" list and the current "Subtotal".
  3. Tap the "Remove item" button for one product.
  4. Observe the "Cart items" list.
  5. Observe the "Subtotal".
Expected Result:
  - The selected item is removed from the cart.
  - The "Cart items" list refreshes and no longer shows the removed product.
  - The "Subtotal" decreases accordingly.
