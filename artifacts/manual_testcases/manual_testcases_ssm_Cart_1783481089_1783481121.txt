Screen: Cart

Test Case 1: Verify Cart screen loads with all key cart components
Description: Validate that the Cart screen presents the core controls and information needed to review selected items and proceed to checkout.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen.
  2. Observe the top area of the screen.
  3. Verify the presence of the Back icon button.
  4. Verify the presence of the Cart title text.
  5. Verify the Cart items list is displayed.
  6. For at least one listed item, verify Product image, Product name, Product price, Quantity stepper, and Remove item button are visible.
  7. Verify the Subtotal text is displayed.
  8. Verify the Checkout button is displayed.
Expected Result:
  - The Cart screen opens successfully.
  - Back, Cart title, Cart items list, item details, Subtotal, and Checkout are visible.
  - Each cart item shows the expected item-specific controls and information.

Test Case 2: Verify navigation using the Back button from Cart
Description: Ensure the user can return to the previous screen from the Cart screen.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen.
  2. Tap the Back icon button.
Expected Result:
  - The app navigates to the previous screen.
  - No crash or UI freeze occurs during navigation.

Test Case 3: Verify cart item list supports scrolling when multiple items exist
Description: Validate that the Cart items list can be scrolled to review all added products.
Steps:
  1. Ensure the cart contains multiple items exceeding the visible area.
  2. Open the Cart screen.
  3. Scroll down through the Cart items list.
  4. Scroll back up.
Expected Result:
  - The Cart items list scrolls smoothly.
  - Additional items become visible as the user scrolls.
  - No overlapping, truncation, or missing controls appear while scrolling.

Test Case 4: Verify tapping Product image opens the related product details
Description: Confirm that selecting a product through its image opens the correct product details view.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with at least one item in the cart.
  2. Note the visible Product name for the first item.
  3. Tap the Product image of that same item.
Expected Result:
  - The app navigates to the corresponding product details screen.
  - The opened product matches the selected cart item.

Test Case 5: Verify tapping Product name opens the related product details
Description: Confirm that selecting a product through its name opens the correct product details view.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with at least one item in the cart.
  2. Note the Product name of a listed item.
  3. Tap the Product name text.
Expected Result:
  - The app navigates to the corresponding product details screen.
  - The displayed product details match the tapped Product name.

Test Case 6: Verify quantity increment updates item quantity and subtotal
Description: Ensure increasing the quantity of a cart item updates the item count and recalculates the cart subtotal.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with at least one item present.
  2. Note the Product price for the selected item.
  3. Note the current quantity shown in the Quantity stepper.
  4. Note the current Subtotal value.
  5. Use the Quantity stepper to increment the quantity by 1.
Expected Result:
  - The item quantity increases by 1.
  - The Subtotal increases accordingly based on the Product price.
  - The updated quantity and subtotal are reflected immediately or after a brief valid refresh.

Test Case 7: Verify quantity decrement updates item quantity and subtotal
Description: Ensure decreasing the quantity of a cart item updates the item count and recalculates the cart subtotal correctly.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with at least one item whose quantity is greater than 1.
  2. Note the current quantity in the Quantity stepper.
  3. Note the current Subtotal value.
  4. Use the Quantity stepper to decrement the quantity by 1.
Expected Result:
  - The item quantity decreases by 1.
  - The Subtotal decreases accordingly.
  - The updated quantity and subtotal are shown correctly.

Test Case 8: Verify quantity stepper does not allow invalid reduction below minimum quantity
Description: Validate that the Quantity stepper prevents a cart item quantity from going below the allowed minimum.
Steps:
  1. Open the Cart screen with an item quantity set to the minimum allowed value, typically 1.
  2. Attempt to decrement the item using the Quantity stepper.
Expected Result:
  - The quantity does not go below the minimum allowed value.
  - The app either disables further decrement or ignores the action gracefully.
  - No incorrect subtotal calculation occurs.
