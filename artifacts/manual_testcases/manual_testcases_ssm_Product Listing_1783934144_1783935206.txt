Screen: Product Listing

Test Case 1: Verify Product Listing screen loads with core browsing controls
Description: Validate that the Product Listing screen displays the expected controls needed to browse and select products from the catalog.
Steps:
  1. Launch the app and navigate to the Product Listing screen.
  2. Observe the top and main content areas of the screen.
  3. Verify the presence of the Search bar.
  4. Verify the presence of the Category filter control.
  5. Verify the presence of the Sort button.
  6. Verify the Product grid is displayed.
  7. Verify at least one Product item is visible in the Product grid.
  8. Verify an Add to cart button is available for a product item or within the listing context.
Expected Result:
  - The Product Listing screen opens successfully.
  - Search, Category filter, Sort, Product grid, Product item, and Add to cart controls are visible and usable.
  - The screen supports product browsing without missing key UI elements.

Test Case 2: Verify product search using valid keyword
Description: Confirm that entering a valid search term in the Search bar updates the product listing to relevant results.
Steps:
  1. Open the Product Listing screen.
  2. Tap the Search bar.
  3. Enter a valid product keyword.
  4. Submit or wait for the search results to refresh.
  5. Review the Product grid results.
Expected Result:
  - The Product grid refreshes based on the entered keyword.
  - Displayed Product items are relevant to the search term.
  - No unrelated or stale results remain if search is applied successfully.

Test Case 3: Verify search behavior with no matching results
Description: Validate that the screen handles unsuccessful searches gracefully when the entered term does not match any product.
Steps:
  1. Open the Product Listing screen.
  2. Tap the Search bar.
  3. Enter a random or invalid search term that should not match any product.
  4. Submit the search.
  5. Observe the Product grid area.
Expected Result:
  - The screen updates to reflect that no matching products were found, or the Product grid becomes empty.
  - The app does not crash or freeze.
  - Search remains editable so the user can try another keyword.

Test Case 4: Verify search field accepts and processes edited text
Description: Ensure the Search bar allows users to modify search text and refresh results accordingly.
Steps:
  1. Open the Product Listing screen.
  2. Tap the Search bar and enter a valid keyword.
  3. Observe the filtered Product grid.
  4. Tap the Search bar again.
  5. Replace the existing text with a different valid keyword.
  6. Submit the updated search.
Expected Result:
  - The Search bar accepts updated text.
  - The Product grid refreshes according to the new keyword.
  - Results from the previous search are replaced by the new relevant results.

Test Case 5: Verify Category filter opens and applies a product filter
Description: Confirm the Category filter can be accessed and that selecting a category narrows the product catalog.
Steps:
  1. Open the Product Listing screen.
  2. Tap Category filter.
  3. Select an available category option.
  4. Confirm the selection if required.
  5. Review the Product grid after filter application.
Expected Result:
  - The Category filter opens successfully.
  - A category can be selected.
  - The Product grid updates to show products belonging to the selected category.
  - The filtered result set is narrower or more specific than the unfiltered catalog.

Test Case 6: Verify Category filter can be changed to another category
Description: Validate that users can switch from one category selection to another and see updated results.
Steps:
  1. Open the Product Listing screen.
  2. Tap Category filter and select one category.
  3. Observe the Product grid results.
  4. Tap Category filter again.
  5. Select a different category.
  6. Review the Product grid.
Expected Result:
  - The category selection changes successfully.
  - The Product grid updates to match the newly selected category.
  - Products from the old category are no longer shown unless they also belong to the new selection.
